Sepsis

  • IV access, cultures, lactate
  • Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid
  • Vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Monitor urine output & lactate trend
  • Pathophysiology: SIRS, endothelial injury, mitochondrial dysfunction
  • Risk factors: immunosuppression, chronic disease, invasive procedures
  • Diagnostic: SOFA, qSOFA, biomarkers, cultures
  • Source control principles
  • Complications: MODS, DIC, septic shock
  • Prognostic indicators: lactate, organ dysfunction scores
  • Calculators: SOFA, qSOFA, MAP resuscitation, antibiotic dosing

Pathogen Overview

  • Staphylococcus aureus: skin flora, MRSA risk, toxin-mediated disease
  • Streptococcus spp.: respiratory flora, beta-hemolytic, virulence factors
  • E. coli: gut flora, ESBL risk, UTIs, bacteremia
  • Pseudomonas: environmental, biofilm, intrinsic resistance
  • Clostridium: spore-forming, anaerobic, toxin-mediated
  • Candida spp.: opportunistic, bloodstream infections
  • Aspergillus spp.: respiratory, immunocompromised hosts
  • Cryptococcus spp.: CNS infection in immunocompromised
  • Influenza: seasonal, respiratory droplets
  • HSV / VZV: latency, reactivation
  • CMV: immunocompromised, disseminated
