Pathogen-Based Guidance

Select therapy according to Gram-stain and culture results. Use narrowest effective spectrum. Adjust for renal/liver function and severity.

Pathogen Overview

Staphylococcus spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide
Risk Factors: Indwelling devices, post-surgical, immunocompromised
Vectors / Reservoirs: Skin, nares
Resistance: MRSA increasing globally; some strains have vancomycin intermediate resistance
Pathophysiology: Toxin-mediated diseases, biofilm formation, invasive infections
Clinical Notes: Causes SSTIs, bacteremia, endocarditis, osteomyelitis

Streptococcus spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide
Risk Factors: Children (group A), elderly, comorbidities
Vectors / Reservoirs: Oropharynx, skin
Resistance: Penicillin generally effective; macrolide resistance varies
Pathophysiology: Exotoxins, post-infectious sequelae
Clinical Notes: Causes pharyngitis, pneumonia, endocarditis, necrotizing fasciitis

Mycobacterium spp.

Prevalence: TB endemic regions worldwide
Risk Factors: Immunocompromised, close contacts, HIV
Vectors / Reservoirs: Aerosol droplets
Resistance: MDR and XDR TB rising
Pathophysiology: Intracellular survival in macrophages, granuloma formation
Clinical Notes: Pulmonary, extrapulmonary TB, chronic infection

Nocardia spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, rare
Risk Factors: Immunocompromised (steroids, transplant)
Vectors / Reservoirs: Soil, environment
Resistance: TMP-SMX sensitive, variable resistance
Pathophysiology: Filamentous, partially acid-fast, opportunistic infection
Clinical Notes: Pulmonary, CNS, disseminated disease

Corynebacterium spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide
Risk Factors: Immunocompromised, invasive devices
Vectors / Reservoirs: Skin, mucosa
Resistance: Some strains resistant to penicillin
Pathophysiology: Exotoxin production in diphtheria; opportunistic infections
Clinical Notes: Diphtheria, bloodstream infections, endocarditis

Bacteroides spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, commensal in gut
Risk Factors: Abdominal surgery, perforated viscus
Vectors / Reservoirs: Human gut
Resistance: Beta-lactamase producers common
Pathophysiology: Anaerobic infection, abscess formation
Clinical Notes: Intra-abdominal abscess, sepsis

Prevotella spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, oral and gut flora
Risk Factors: Dental procedures, aspiration
Vectors / Reservoirs: Mouth, GI tract
Resistance: Usually susceptible to metronidazole
Pathophysiology: Anaerobic infection
Clinical Notes: Aspiration pneumonia, oral infections

Clostridium spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, environmental spores
Risk Factors: Trauma, antibiotic use, immunocompromised
Vectors / Reservoirs: Soil, gut flora
Resistance: Some species resistant to clindamycin
Pathophysiology: Toxin-mediated, anaerobic infection
Clinical Notes: C. difficile colitis, gas gangrene, tetanus

Actinomyces spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, rare
Risk Factors: Poor dental hygiene, immunocompromised
Vectors / Reservoirs: Oral cavity, GI tract
Resistance: Penicillin sensitive
Pathophysiology: Chronic granulomatous infection
Clinical Notes: Cervicofacial, thoracic, abdominal infections

Burkholderia spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, often in CF patients
Risk Factors: Cystic fibrosis, immunocompromised
Vectors / Reservoirs: Environmental soil and water
Resistance: Multidrug resistance common
Pathophysiology: Gram-negative bacilli, opportunistic pathogen
Clinical Notes: Pulmonary infections, sepsis

Candida spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, hospital-acquired common
Risk Factors: Central lines, ICU, immunosuppression
Vectors / Reservoirs: GI tract, mucosa
Resistance: Fluconazole resistance increasing
Pathophysiology: Yeast infection, biofilm formation
Clinical Notes: Candidemia, mucosal infections

Aspergillus spp.

Prevalence: Worldwide, immunocompromised hosts
Risk Factors: Neutropenia, transplant, steroids
Vectors / Reservoirs: Environmental spores
Resistance: Azole resistance rising
Pathophysiology: Mold infection, angioinvasion
Clinical Notes: Pulmonary, disseminated aspergillosis

HSV / VZV

Prevalence: Worldwide
Risk Factors: Immunosuppression, neonates
Vectors / Reservoirs: Human mucosa, secretions
Resistance: Rare acyclovir resistance in immunocompromised
Pathophysiology: Latent viral infection, reactivation
Clinical Notes: Mucocutaneous lesions, encephalitis

Influenza A/B

Prevalence: Seasonal, worldwide
Risk Factors: Elderly, comorbidities, children
Vectors / Reservoirs: Respiratory droplets
Resistance: Oseltamivir resistance rare
Pathophysiology: Respiratory viral infection
Clinical Notes: Pneumonia, exacerbation of chronic illness
