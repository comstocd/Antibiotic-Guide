Antibiotic & Infection Guide

Quick Tips:
  • Start empiric therapy within 1 hour for sepsis.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture & sensitivity.
  • Consider organ function for dosing adjustments.
  • Duration depends on site, severity, and clinical response.
  • Review local resistance patterns before therapy.
  • Document rationale for escalation/de-escalation.

Head & Neck Infections

  • Sinusitis: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID x5–7d
  • Otitis media: Amoxicillin 80–90 mg/kg/day PO divided q12h
  • Dental abscess: Clindamycin 300 mg PO q6–8h or Amoxicillin-clavulanate

Respiratory Tract Infections

  • CAP: Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV q24h + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO daily
  • HAP/VAP: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h, Cefepime 2 g IV q8h ± Vancomycin

Urinary Tract / Pyelonephritis

  • Uncomplicated: Nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID x5 days
  • Complicated / Pyelonephritis: Ceftriaxone 1 g IV q24h or Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID

Gastrointestinal / Intra-abdominal

  • Diverticulitis mild: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID + Metronidazole 500 mg PO TID x7–10d
  • Diverticulitis severe/hospitalized: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h
  • Peritonitis: Broad-spectrum with anaerobic and Gram-negative coverage

Skin & Soft Tissue

  • Cellulitis: Cefazolin 1–2 g IV q8h or Vancomycin if MRSA risk
  • Necrotizing fasciitis: Broad-spectrum IV therapy including Vancomycin + Piperacillin-tazobactam

Bone & Joint Infections

  • Osteomyelitis: Nafcillin 2 g IV q4h (MSSA) / Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h (MRSA) ± Gram-negative coverage if post-trauma
  • Septic arthritis: Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV q24h ± Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h

Sepsis

  • Obtain blood cultures before antibiotics if not delaying therapy.
  • Start broad-spectrum empiric antibiotics within 1 hour.
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
  • Monitor vitals, urine output, lactate.
  • Escalate to ICU for vasopressors if persistent hypotension.
  • Pathophysiology: dysregulated host response, cytokine storm, organ dysfunction.
  • Complications: ARDS, AKI, DIC, shock.
  • Approach: identify source, source control, pathogen-targeted therapy, supportive care.

Fungal Infections

  • Candida bloodstream: Fluconazole 400 mg IV daily; adjust for renal
  • Invasive Aspergillus: Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV q12h loading → 4 mg/kg q12h
  • Crytococcus: Amphotericin B 0.7–1 mg/kg IV + Flucytosine 100 mg/kg PO daily

Viral Infections

  • HSV: Acyclovir 5–10 mg/kg IV q8h
  • CMV: Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h
  • Influenza: Oseltamivir 75 mg PO BID x5 days
