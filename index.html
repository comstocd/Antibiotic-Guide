Basic ID Approach Tips:

  • Obtain detailed history (travel, exposure, immunization).
  • Perform thorough physical examination for source clues.
  • Collect appropriate cultures before antibiotics when possible.
  • Start empiric therapy based on likely pathogens and severity.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture results and patient response.
  • Consider host factors: age, comorbidities, immunosuppression.

Pathogen Overview

  • MSSA: treat with Nafcillin/Oxacillin
  • MRSA: treat with Vancomycin

  • Resistance: beta-lactamase, MRSA mechanisms
  • Gram-positive cocci, chains
  • Causes: pharyngitis, pneumonia, bacteremia
  • Penicillin-sensitive; some species show macrolide resistance

  • Causes: UTI, endocarditis, intra-abdominal infection
  • Resistant strains: VRE (vancomycin-resistant)
  • Gram-negative rod, facultative anaerobe
  • Common cause of UTI, bacteremia, intra-abdominal infections
  • ESBL resistance increasing globally
  • Pathogenesis: adhesins, endotoxins
  • Risk factors: catheterization, hospitalization, immunosuppression
  • Gram-negative rod
  • Causes pneumonia, UTIs, bacteremia
  • ESBL and carbapenem resistance emerging
  • Virulence: capsule, siderophores
  • Risk factors: diabetes, chronic lung disease
