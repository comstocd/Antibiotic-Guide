Basic ID Approach Tips:

  • Assess severity, comorbidities, and immunocompetence.
  • Obtain cultures prior to starting antibiotics if possible.
  • Start empiric therapy based on likely pathogens and infection site.
  • Adjust antibiotics according to culture results and susceptibilities.
  • Consider source control: drain abscesses, remove infected devices.

Infections by Anatomic Site

  • Sinusitis: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID 5–7d
  • Mastoiditis: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h 10–14d
  • Otitis media: Amoxicillin 500 mg PO q8h 5–7d
  • CAP: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO day 1 then 250 mg daily 5–7d
  • HAP/VAP: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h 7–14d, add Vancomycin for MRSA risk
  • Uncomplicated UTI: Nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID 5–7d
  • Pyelonephritis: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO q12h 7–10d
  • Diverticulitis: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO q12h + Metronidazole 500 mg PO q8h 7–10d
  • Appendicitis post-op prophylaxis: Cefazolin 2 g IV pre-op single dose
  • Cellulitis: Cefazolin 2 g IV q8h 7–10d
  • Necrotizing fasciitis: Broad-spectrum (Meropenem + Vancomycin + Clindamycin) 14–21d
  • Osteomyelitis: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h 6 weeks
  • Septic arthritis: Vancomycin + Ceftriaxone 2–4 weeks

Sepsis

  • Obtain cultures before antibiotics if possible, but do not delay therapy.
  • Start broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour.
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus if hypotensive or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
  • Vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg if hypotension persists.
  • Measure lactate; repeat if initial >2 mmol/L.
  • Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by dysregulated host response to infection.
  • Monitor SOFA/qSOFA scores to guide severity and prognosis.
  • Address source control: remove infected lines, drain abscesses, debride necrotic tissue.
  • Adjust antibiotic regimen based on culture and sensitivities.
  • Monitor for complications: DIC, ARDS, AKI, multi-organ failure.

Pathogen-Based Guidance

  • Staphylococcus aureus: MSSA (Nafcillin), MRSA (Vancomycin)
  • Streptococcus spp.: Penicillin, Ceftriaxone
  • Enterococcus spp.: Ampicillin or Vancomycin
  • E. coli, Klebsiella: Ceftriaxone, Piperacillin-tazobactam
  • Pseudomonas: Piperacillin-tazobactam, Ceftazidime
  • Burkholderia: Ceftazidime, Meropenem
  • Bacteroides fragilis: Metronidazole or Piperacillin-tazobactam
  • Clostridium spp.: Oral Vancomycin or Metronidazole
  • Actinomyces: Penicillin G
  • Chlamydia, Legionella, Mycoplasma: Macrolides or Tetracyclines
  • Nocardia: TMP-SMX

Tickborne Illnesses

  • Gram-negative spirochete
  • Early: erythema migrans, fever, arthralgia
  • Treatment: Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID 10–21 days
  • Intracellular Gram-negative bacterium
  • Symptoms: fever, malaise, cytopenias
  • Treatment: Doxycycline 100 mg PO/IV BID 10–14 days
  • Intraerythrocytic protozoa
  • Symptoms: hemolysis, fever, fatigue
  • Treatment: Atovaquone + Azithromycin for 7–10 days
  • Obligate intracellular Gram-negative bacterium
  • Symptoms: fever, rash, headache
  • Treatment: Doxycycline 100 mg PO/IV BID 7–14 days

Viral Encephalopathies

  • Flavivirus transmitted by mosquitoes
  • Symptoms: fever, headache, neuroinvasive disease in some
  • Treatment: Supportive care, no antiviral approved
  • Alphavirus transmitted by mosquitoes
  • Symptoms: high fever, severe neurological disease
  • Treatment: Supportive care, ICU-level management
  • Flavivirus transmitted by Culex mosquitoes
  • Symptoms: fever, meningitis/encephalitis
  • Treatment: Supportive care, vaccination preventive
