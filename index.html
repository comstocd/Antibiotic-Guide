Pathogen Overview

Streptococcus spp.
  • Prevalence: Worldwide; common cause of pharyngitis, pneumonia, endocarditis
  • Risk Factors: Elderly, immunocompromised, chronic illness
  • Vectors: Person-to-person via droplets
  • Resistance: Penicillin generally effective; macrolide resistance increasing
  • Pathophysiology: Capsule prevents phagocytosis; hemolysins damage tissue
  • Clinical Notes: Site-specific therapy recommended
Staphylococcus aureus
  • Prevalence: Skin, soft tissue infections; MRSA increasing worldwide
  • Risk Factors: Hospitalization, IV catheters, diabetes
  • Vectors: Person-to-person, fomites
  • Resistance: MRSA resistant to beta-lactams
  • Pathophysiology: Toxins, biofilm formation
  • Clinical Notes: Culture-directed therapy essential
