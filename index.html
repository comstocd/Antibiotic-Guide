Antibiotic & Infection Guide

Quick Tips:
  • Start empiric therapy within 1 hour for sepsis.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture & sensitivity.
  • Consider organ function for dosing adjustments.
  • Duration depends on site, severity, and clinical response.
  • Review local resistance patterns before therapy.
  • Document rationale for escalation/de-escalation.

Head & Neck Infections

  • Sinusitis: Amoxicillin-clavulanate
  • Otitis media: Amoxicillin
  • Dental abscess: Clindamycin or Amoxicillin-clavulanate

Respiratory Tract

  • CAP: Ceftriaxone + Macrolide or Monotherapy Respiratory Fluoroquinolone
  • HAP/VAP: Piperacillin-tazobactam, Cefepime, or Meropenem ± Vancomycin

Urinary Tract / Pyelonephritis

  • Uncomplicated: Nitrofurantoin or Trimethoprim-SMX
  • Complicated / Pyelonephritis: Ceftriaxone or Ciprofloxacin

Gastrointestinal / Intra-abdominal

  • Diverticulitis (mild): Ciprofloxacin + Metronidazole
  • Diverticulitis (severe/hospitalized): Piperacillin-tazobactam
  • Peritonitis: Broad-spectrum coverage with anaerobic and Gram-negative coverage

Skin & Soft Tissue

  • Cellulitis: Cefazolin or Vancomycin if MRSA risk
  • Necrotizing fasciitis: Broad-spectrum including MRSA, anaerobic coverage

Bone & Joint Infections

  • Osteomyelitis: Nafcillin / Vancomycin ± Gram-negative coverage if post-trauma
  • Septic arthritis: Ceftriaxone ± Vancomycin

Sepsis

  • Obtain cultures before antibiotics if not delaying therapy.
  • Start broad-spectrum empiric antibiotics within 1 hour.
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
  • Monitor vitals, urine output, lactate.
  • Escalate to ICU for vasopressors if persistent hypotension.
  • Pathophysiology: dysregulated host response, cytokine storm, organ dysfunction.
  • Complications: ARDS, AKI, DIC, shock.
  • Approach: identify source, source control, pathogen-targeted therapy, supportive care.

Fungal Infections

  • Candida spp.: fluconazole; echinocandins for resistant species
  • Aspergillus spp.: voriconazole; amphotericin B if refractory
  • Cryptococcus: amphotericin B + flucytosine, then fluconazole

Viral Infections

  • Influenza A/B: oseltamivir 75mg PO BID x5 days
  • HSV 1/2: acyclovir 400mg PO TID or IV if severe
  • CMV: ganciclovir IV, valganciclovir PO maintenance
