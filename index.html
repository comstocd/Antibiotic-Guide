Sepsis

  • Obtain IV access, cultures, lactate
  • Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension / lactate ≥4 mmol/L
  • Vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Monitor urine output and lactate trend
  • Pathophysiology: SIRS, endothelial injury, mitochondrial dysfunction
  • Risk factors: immunosuppression, chronic disease, invasive procedures
  • Diagnostic approach: SOFA, qSOFA, biomarkers, cultures
  • Source control principles
  • Complications: MODS, DIC, septic shock
  • Prognostic indicators: lactate, organ dysfunction scores

Pathogen Overview

  • Staphylococcus aureus: skin flora, MRSA risk, toxin-mediated disease
  • Streptococcus spp.: upper respiratory flora, virulence factors, beta-hemolysis
  • E. coli: gut flora, ESBL risk, UTIs, bacteremia
  • Pseudomonas: environmental reservoirs, intrinsic resistance, biofilm formation
  • Clostridium: spore-forming, anaerobic, C. difficile colitis
  • Candida spp.: opportunistic, hospital-acquired bloodstream infections
  • Aspergillus spp.: respiratory, immunocompromised hosts
  • Cryptococcus spp.: CNS infections in immunocompromised
  • Influenza: seasonal, respiratory droplets, antiviral therapy
  • HSV / VZV: latency, reactivation, antiviral therapy
  • CMV: immunocompromised, antiviral therapy

Fungal Infections

  • Candida: fluconazole, echinocandins

Viral Infections

  • Influenza: oseltamivir
  • HSV / VZV: acyclovir / valacyclovir
  • CMV: ganciclovir / valganciclovir
  • RSV: supportive / ribavirin in severe cases
