Pathogen Overview

Streptococcus spp.
  • Worldwide prevalence
  • Risk: Immunocompromised, post-viral
  • Resistance: Macrolide resistance increasing
  • Pathophysiology: Exotoxin, capsule
Staphylococcus aureus
  • Worldwide prevalence
  • Risk: Catheter, hospitalization
  • Resistance: MRSA
  • Biofilm and toxin mediated
Mycobacterium spp.
  • TB endemic regions
  • Risk: HIV, crowded living
  • MDR/XDR strains rising
  • Slow-growing, intracellular
Nocardia spp.
  • Soil-borne opportunist
  • Risk: Immunocompromised
  • TMP-SMX usually effective
  • Aerobic filamentous rods
Corynebacterium spp.
  • Skin/mucosa colonizer
  • Risk: Immunocompromised
  • Variable resistance
  • Rod-shaped
Bacteroides, Prevotella, Actinomyces, Clostridium
  • Normal flora/opportunistic infections
  • Risk: GI surgery, aspiration
  • Resistance patterns variable
  • Rod-shaped, anaerobic
Burkholderia spp.
  • Soil, water exposure
  • Risk: CF patients, immunocompromised
  • Highly resistant
  • Gram-negative rod
Intracellular Pathogens
  • Chlamydia, Rickettsia, Legionella
  • Risk: Exposure to vectors, endemic regions
  • Macrolide / Doxycycline sensitive
  • Obligate intracellular
Fungi
  • Candida, Aspergillus, Cryptococcus
  • Risk: Immunocompromised
  • Resistance emerging
  • Yeast or mold
Viruses
  • HSV, VZV, Influenza, CMV
  • Risk: Immunosuppressed, exposure
  • Virus-specific antiviral therapy
  • Replicate intracellularly
