Basic ID Approach Tips:

  • Obtain detailed history (travel, exposure, immunization).
  • Perform thorough physical examination for source clues.
  • Collect appropriate cultures before antibiotics when possible.
  • Start empiric therapy based on likely pathogens and severity.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture results and patient response.
  • Consider host factors: age, comorbidities, immunosuppression.

Tickborne Illnesses

  • Pathogen: Borrelia burgdorferi
  • Drug: Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID
  • Duration: 10–21 days depending on stage
  • Pathogen: Anaplasma phagocytophilum
  • Drug: Doxycycline 100 mg PO/IV BID
  • Duration: 10–14 days
  • Pathogen: Babesia microti
  • Drug: Atovaquone + Azithromycin or Clindamycin + Quinine
  • Duration: 7–10 days (mild), 10+ days (severe)
  • Examples: West Nile Virus, Eastern Equine Encephalitis, Powassan Virus
  • Supportive care; no specific antiviral therapy
