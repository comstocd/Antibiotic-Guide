Pathogen Overview

Gram-Positive Cocci
  • Streptococcus: Pharyngeal colonization; endocarditis risk; generally penicillin-susceptible.
  • Staphylococcus aureus: Skin colonization; MRSA risk varies by region; biofilm formation; beta-lactam resistance common.
  • Enterococcus: GI tract; vancomycin-resistant strains increasing; nosocomial infections.
Gram-Positive Rods
  • Mycobacterium: TB: airborne; slow-growing; multidrug resistance increasing.
  • Corynebacterium: Skin/mucous; mostly non-pathogenic; C. diphtheriae produces toxin.
  • Nocardia: Soil; immunocompromised; pulmonary/CNS infections.
  • Clostridium: Anaerobic, spore-forming; tetanus, botulism, gas gangrene.
  • Actinomyces: Oral/abdominal flora; chronic suppurative infections.
Gram-Negative Cocci
  • Neisseria meningitidis: Respiratory spread; meningitis outbreaks; capsule typing important.
  • Neisseria gonorrhoeae: STI; increasing cephalosporin resistance.
  • Moraxella catarrhalis: Respiratory flora; beta-lactamase common.
Gram-Negative Rods
  • Enterobacteriaceae: GI tract; ESBL production common; nosocomial risk.
  • Burkholderia: CF patients; intrinsic resistance.
  • Bacteroides: Anaerobic; intra-abdominal infections; beta-lactamase producers.
  • Prevotella: Oral anaerobe; abscess formation.
Intracellular Pathogens
  • Chlamydia spp.: Intracellular; STIs; respiratory infections.
  • Legionella: Water reservoirs; pneumonia; intracellular growth.
  • Rickettsia: Vector-borne; endothelial infection.
  • Mycoplasma: Respiratory infections; no cell wall.
  • Coxiella burnetii: Q fever; zoonotic; chronic endocarditis in susceptible patients.
Fungi
  • Candida: Endogenous colonization; immunocompromised at risk; resistance varies by species.
  • Aspergillus: Environmental; invasive in neutropenia.
  • Cryptococcus: Soil; HIV patients; CNS tropism.
  • Histoplasma/Coccidioides: Endemic mycoses; inhalation; chronic pulmonary risk.
Viruses
  • Influenza: Respiratory droplet spread; seasonal; high-risk in elderly/pregnant.
  • HSV/VZV: Latent infection; reactivation common.
  • CMV: Immunocompromised risk; latent infection.
  • RSV, Adenovirus: Respiratory pathogens; inpatient concern mainly in immunocompromised/infants.
