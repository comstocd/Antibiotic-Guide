Antibiotic & Infection Guide

Quick Tips:
  • Start empiric therapy within 1 hour for sepsis.
  • Adjust antibiotics based on culture & sensitivity.
  • Consider organ function for dosing adjustments.
  • Duration depends on site, severity, and clinical response.

Head & Neck Infections

  • Sinusitis: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID 5–7d
  • Mastoiditis: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h 10–14d
  • Otitis media: Amoxicillin 500 mg PO q8h 5–7d

Respiratory Tract

  • CAP: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO day 1 then 250 mg daily 5–7d
  • HAP/VAP: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h 7–14d, add Vancomycin for MRSA risk

Urinary Tract / Pyelonephritis

  • Uncomplicated UTI: Nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID 5–7d
  • Pyelonephritis: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO q12h 7–10d

Gastrointestinal / Intra-abdominal

  • Diverticulitis: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO q12h + Metronidazole 500 mg PO q8h 7–10d
  • Appendicitis post-op prophylaxis: Cefazolin 2 g IV pre-op single dose

Skin & Soft Tissue

  • Cellulitis: Cefazolin 2 g IV q8h 7–10d
  • Necrotizing fasciitis: Broad-spectrum (Meropenem + Vancomycin + Clindamycin) 14–21d

Bone & Joint

  • Osteomyelitis: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h 6 weeks
  • Septic arthritis: Vancomycin + Ceftriaxone 2–4 weeks

Sepsis

  • Obtain cultures before antibiotics if possible, but do not delay therapy.
  • Start broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour.
  • Fluid resuscitation: 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus if hypotensive or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
  • Vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg if hypotension persists.
  • Measure lactate; repeat if initial >2 mmol/L.
  • Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by dysregulated host response to infection.
  • Monitor SOFA/qSOFA scores to guide severity and prognosis.
  • Address source control: remove infected lines, drain abscesses, debride necrotic tissue.
  • Adjust antibiotic regimen based on culture and sensitivities.
  • Monitor for complications: DIC, ARDS, AKI, multi-organ failure.
